Notes

1Energies in eV above X 1Σ+(v=0) of CO+, all obtained as resonances in electron scattering experiments.
2Resonances with the vibrational level v-0,1,2 of a3Π of CO, ascribed to a state of CO- with the same potential function as a3Π.
3From D00(CO) and the electron affinities of CO (-1.5 eV) and 0 (+1.465 eV).
4Progression of six resonances, observed in electron transmission Sanche and Schulz, 1972 and electroionization Carbonneau and Marmet, 1973 experiments; vibrational numbering uncertain. Its "grandparent" is assumed to be the A 2Π state of CO+, with the additional two electrons in a Rydberg orbital.
5Resonances in the excitation functions of CO, B 1Σ+ and b 3Σ+ Mazeau, Gresteau, et al., 1972, and in the electron transmission current Sanche and Schulz, 1972.
6Sharp resonance (width ~0.045 eV); v'=0 and 1. The parent states are b 3Σ+ and B 1Σ+ of CO. Decay into CO(X 1Σ, a 3Π, a' 3Σ, A 1Π) + e-, C + O-, and C- + O: see also Swanson, Celotta, et al., 1975.
7Very broad resonance, vibrational structure barely detectable.
